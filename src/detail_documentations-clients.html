Overview

This documentation is designed to enhance your understanding of your site and Webflow.

It will be updated based on your requests and as your site evolves.

Please feel free to share it with anyone involved in modifying your Webflow site, to ensure that tasks are executed in the most optimized manner and to prevent any errors or the creation of bugs.

  • Quick links to the most useful and frequently requested official Webflow documentation (in English),

Warning

If you make changes or have other developers intervene, this documentation may become outdated. Keeping the documentation current will facilitate the involvement of future service providers on your site. 😉

Here are some useful information we think you should now 😉

Backups and undo on Webflow

You can undo a change by using the cmd + z (apple users) or ctrl + z (windows and linux users) keyboard shortcut.

To redo, use cmd + shift + z (apple users) or ctrl + shift + z (windows and linux users)

Webflow also have a backup system. It will automatically create a backup at some times or when detecting changes on the website. You can also create manual backups when you want to save a particular state from your Webflow, before doing a major change for example.

You can either restore a backup or just preview it in a read-only link to copy-paste things or just see how it was.
